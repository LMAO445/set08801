Geography

Location: Albania is in the Balkan Peninsula, bordered by Montenegro, Kosovo, North Macedonia, and Greece. It has a coastline along the Adriatic and Ionian Seas.

Capital City: Tirana_A lively and colorful city with a mix of Ottoman, Italian, and Soviet influences.

Culture & People

Ancient Civilization: Home to the Illyrians, an ancient people who lived in the Balkans.

Ottoman Rule (15th-20th century): Albania was part of the Ottoman Empire for over 400 years.

Independence (1912): Declared independence from the Ottoman Empire.

Communist Era (1944-1991): Led by dictator Enver Hoxha, Albania was isolated from the world under a strict communist regime.

Modern Albania: A democratic country since the 1990s, now developing rapidly with a growing tourism industry.

Economy

Tourism: One of the fastest-growing sectors, with beautiful beaches, mountains, and historical sites attracting visitors..

Agriculture: Olives, grapes, citrus fruits, and medicinal herbs.
